# Vehicle Lifecycle

## Purpose

This document describes how a vehicle moves through the AutoConnect platform from onboarding to long-term ownership monitoring.

---

## Step 1 — Vehicle Registration

The owner adds a vehicle to the platform.

Collected information:

- VIN
- make
- model
- year
- mileage

---

## Step 2 — Device Connection

Vehicle is connected using:

- OBD-II device
- telematics device
- future OEM integrations

---

## Step 3 — Telemetry Collection

Examples:

- engine temperature
- battery voltage
- RPM
- EV battery health
- charging cycles
- fuel consumption

---

## Step 4 — Diagnostics

Telemetry is analyzed.

Generated outputs:

- fault detection
- diagnostic classification
- risk indicators

---

## Step 5 — Vehicle Intelligence

The platform generates:

- health score
- risk level
- maintenance recommendations
- ownership insights

---

## Step 6 — Driver Analytics

The platform evaluates:

- hard braking
- overspeed events
- rapid accelerations

Generated outputs:

- driver score
- behavior classification

---

## Step 7 — Insurance Intelligence

Driver and vehicle risk data are converted into:

- insurance risk profile
- premium multiplier estimate

---

## Step 8 — Maintenance Prediction

The platform predicts:

- oil service requirements
- inspection deadlines
- tire service requirements

---

## Step 9 — Service Recommendation

Suitable providers are ranked using:

- rating
- distance
- specialization
- estimated price

---

## Step 10 — Ownership Intelligence

The platform tracks:

- fuel expenses
- maintenance costs
- insurance costs
- parking expenses
- fines

---

## End Goal

Create a complete digital lifecycle for vehicle ownership, maintenance and operational decision-making.
